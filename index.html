Marine Drive: The Queen's Necklace

Marine Drive is a scenic promenade in South Mumbai that offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea. The 3.6 km long road curves along the coastline and is known as the Queen's Necklace because of the way the streetlights along the road resemble a string of pearls at night. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, especially during the monsoon season when the waves crash against the sea wall and spray water over the road.

If you're looking for a romantic spot to watch the sunset or just want to take a leisurely stroll, Marine Drive is the perfect place. There are also plenty of food stalls and cafes nearby where you can grab a snack or a cup of tea while enjoying the view.

Gateway of India: Mumbai's Iconic Landmark

The Gateway of India is a triumphal arch and monument located in South Mumbai that was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911. It's one of Mumbai's most iconic landmarks and is a popular spot for tourists to take photos and enjoy the view of the Arabian Sea.

From the Gateway of India, you can take a ferry to the nearby Elephanta Caves, which are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and feature ancient rock-cut temples and sculptures. There are also plenty of street vendors and souvenir shops nearby where you can pick up a memento of your visit.
